When Free Makes Sense

Free transcription apps are legitimate tools‚Äînot scams or trials. They work offline, protect your privacy, and cost nothing. But they trade speed and features for zero cost.

Choose free transcription if:

  • You transcribe 1-2 hours per month or less
  • Speed doesn't matter‚Äîyou can wait 30-40 minutes per hour of audio
  • You only need basic TXT output
  • You're testing transcription before committing
  • Budget is absolutely zero (students, hobbyists)

Free isn't worth it if:

  • You transcribe 10+ hours total (time cost > $4 paid app)
  • You need batch processing for multiple files
  • You need subtitle formats (SRT/VTT)
  • Your time is valuable‚Äî6x speed difference adds up fast

Common Questions

Are these actually free forever?

Yes. MacWhisper, WhisperMate, and AI Transcription are genuinely free with no trials, ads, or hidden subscriptions. They're passion projects or freemium models where the free tier works indefinitely.

Is offline transcription private?

Yes. All three apps process audio locally on your Mac. Nothing gets uploaded to servers. Your recordings never leave your device.

Why is MacWhisper slow compared to paid apps?

MacWhisper uses an unoptimized version of OpenAI's Whisper model. Paid apps like Scriber Pro optimize processing with custom builds and Apple Silicon acceleration. You're trading speed for zero cost.

Can I use these for commercial work?

Check each app's license, but generally yes. There are no restrictions on using the transcripts commercially. Just verify the specific license terms.

What about macOS built-in dictation?

macOS dictation is for real-time speech-to-text, not file transcription. It doesn't process existing audio/video files‚Äîyou'd have to play them back while dictation listens, which is impractical.
